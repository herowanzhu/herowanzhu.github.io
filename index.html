About He Zhu

    I am an assistant professor at the Department of Computer Science at Rutgers University-New Brunswick. My research focuses on improving software reliability and security via machine learning, program analysis, and program synthesis with a special focus on type systems, automated reasoning, and formal verification.
    My current research interest lies at the intersection between deep learning and symbolic reasoning. On the one hand, I am working on how to apply deep learning to develop correct-by-construction large-size software and cyber-physical systems. On the onther hand, I am investigating how symbolic reasoning can make deep learning more efficient, reliable and correct (by construction).

    I obtained my PhD in Computer Science at Purdue University, advised by Prof. Suresh Jagannathan.

    I am looking for Ph.D. students and undergraduate researchers with backgournd and interests in symbolic reasoning, deep learning, program analysis, verification and synthesis. If you are interested in working with me, please send me an email with your CV.

Research Interests

    I am interested in leveraging symbolic reasoning and deep Learning techniques for building trustworthy software and self-learning systems.
    I contributed to the areas of Programming Languages, Machine Learning, Formal Methods and Autonomous Systems.
    Current projects include:
    • Program analysis, synthesis and verification applied to deep learning.
    • Deep learning applied to program analysis, synthesis, verification and system construction.
    • Formal verification meets reinforcement learning.
    • Distributed system verification.
