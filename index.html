About He Zhu

    I am an assistant professor at the Department of Computer Science at Rutgers University-New Brunswick.

    My research focuses on improving software safety, correctness, and trustworthiness via program analysis, verification, synthesis, and machine learning. I am particularly interested in formal methods and automated reasoning.

    My current research interest lies at the intersection between deep learning and symbolic reasoning. On the one hand, I am working on how to apply deep learning to improve software system safety and correctness. On the other hand, I am investigating how symbolic reasoning can make deep learning more efficient, reliable, and trustworthy. Besides deep learning systems, I also enjoy applying formal verification to real-world distributed systems.

    I obtained my PhD in Computer Science at Purdue University, advised by Prof. Suresh Jagannathan.

    I am looking for Ph.D. students and undergraduate researchers with backgournd and interests in formal verification, symbolic reasoning, deep learning, program analysis, verification and synthesis. If you are interested in working with me, please send me an email with your CV.

Research Interests

    I am interested in applying formal methods (formal verification) to build large-scale trustworthy software systems. My current research focues on leveraging symbolic reasoning to construct trustworthy deep learning systems and formally reasoning about real-world distributed systems.
    I contributed to the areas of Formal Methods, Programming Languages and Machine Learning.
    Current projects include:
    • Program analysis, synthesis and verification applied to deep learning.
    • Deep learning applied to program analysis, synthesis, and verification.
    • Formal verification meets reinforcement learning.
    • Formal verification meets distributed systems.
